Voice and tone

This guide defines how Black Mountain Honey communicates across all channels: YouTube, social media, email, and the website. It should also inform visual and design decisions where appropriate.

Brand personality archetype

Imagine an experienced beekeeper who lives down the road. You can call them any time with a question—even a stupid one—and they’ll give you a straight answer based on years of real experience. They’ll tell you what works, what doesn’t, and probably share a story about that time everything went horribly wrong. They’re not trying to sell you anything, but if they do recommend something, you trust it because they’ve actually used it. That’s Laurence. That’s Black Mountain Honey.

Core voice: Who is Laurence?

Black Mountain Honey’s voice is Laurence. He’s not a brand persona invented by a marketing team—he’s a real person with a distinct way of communicating that audiences have grown to trust. Understanding Laurence is the foundation of everything else.

Laurence is

  • A knowledgeable beekeeper who talks like your mate, not a textbook
  • Self-deprecating and honest about mistakes (his nephew left the door open; he got stung on the nose)
  • Genuinely enthusiastic without being performative—when he tastes honey, you believe him
  • Direct and practical—e tells you what works and what doesn't, no hedging
  • Protective of his audience—he refuses to recommend products he hasn't tested, calls out fake honey, and warns against bad advice
  • Occasionally sweary but never aggressive—a well-placed “bloody hell” when something goes wrong

Laurence is NOT

  • Corporate, slick, or overly polished
  • Condescending or gatekeeping about beekeeping knowledge
  • Afraid to admit when he doesn’t know something
  • Interested in drama for drama’s sake—he uses attention-grabbing hooks but always delivers value

Attributes

Authentically educational

Laurence teaches by showing, not lecturing. He explains the “why” behind everything and isn’t afraid to go deep on technical topics while keeping the language accessible. Sounds like:

The reason they say to never go inside your colonies over winter is that you’re going to let the cold in, add in loads of risk by pulling through the frames, and maybe you’re going to damage your queen. There’s no real reason to ever go in. So, why have I just taken the roof off? The reason I’m doing this is so you don’t have to do this at home.

Key characteristics

  • Explains reasoning, not just instructions
  • Acknowledges what can go wrong
  • Positions himself as testing things for the audience
  • Uses phrases like “Let me show you,” “Here’s what I do,” “This is the way that I do it.”

Self-deprecating and honest

Laurence doesn’t pretend to be perfect. He shares his mistakes, admits when he’s nervous, and uses humor to keep things grounded. Sounds like:

Anyone else is just kind of crawling out of bed, but I do—I do go to bed very early now.

I genuinely can’t quite get over this one. I said to him once, make sure you close the door. I said it twice, make sure you close the door. And he repeated it back and said, I would definitely close the door.

That was very, very painful. But this colony here…

Key characteristics

  • Laughs at himself before anyone else can
  • Shares real-world chaos (equipment failures, stings, mistakes)
  • Uses self-deprecation to build relatability, not to undermine expertise
  • British humor: dry, understated, occasionally dark

Reassuring and supportive

The brand positions itself as a guide through the beekeeping journey. No question is too basic, and ongoing support is emphasized constantly. Sounds like:

We are here for you every step of the way.

There are NO silly questions and you can ask as many as you like.

Starting out in beekeeping can be tough! There are so many things that can go wrong. At Black Mountain Honey, we keep things simple.

Key characteristics

  • Acknowledges that beekeeping is hard
  • Emphasises unlimited, ongoing support
  • Uses “we” and “you” conversationally
  • Celebrates customer successes genuinely

Direct and no-nonsense

Laurence doesn’t hedge or waffle. He gives clear opinions and isn’t afraid to say when something is rubbish. Sounds like:

Do not inspect your colonies in winter. There is no benefit whatsoever.

We don’t cut corners!

I’ve done videos on that. I kind of settled on that for a number of years really thinking it’s there or thereabouts, but it’s not perfect.

Key characteristics

  • States opinions clearly and confidently
  • Backs up claims with experience and evidence
  • Willing to change his mind publicly when he learns better
  • Uses phrases like “the only way,” “this is what works,” and “don’t do this”

Passionate without being preachy

Laurence genuinely loves bees and beekeeping. His enthusiasm is infectious but never feels forced or performative. Sounds like:

It is one of the most rewarding moments in beekeeping, seeing the work come together just like this.

I do miss the beekeeping. The smell of the colony is just fantastic.

That is perfection.

Key characteristics

  • Shows genuine emotion (excitement, satisfaction, frustration)
  • Uses sensory language when describing honey and beekeeping
  • Enthusiasm comes through naturally, not via exclamation marks
  • Balances passion with pragmatism

Tone variations by channel

The core voice stays consistent, but the tone shifts depending on context.

YouTube videos

Tone: Conversational, educational, occasionally dramatic for hooks

Characteristics

  • Opens with attention-grabbing statements or questions
  • Talks directly to camera as if to a friend
  • Uses “let me show you” framing frequently
  • Acknowledges mistakes and problems in real-time
  • Includes asides and tangents that feel natural
  • Signs off with clear calls to action (subscribe, check out the course, visit the website)

Example hook styles

They say you should never ever go into your beehives in winter to do any sort of inspection. But today, I’m going to inspect that colony there…

I genuinely can’t believe he’s done this.

YouTube thumbnails and titles

Tone: Bold, urgent, clickable—but honest

Characteristics

  • Capital letters for emphasis (“10 YEARS UNTOUCHED!”)
  • Dramatic language that delivers on the promise
  • Laurence’s face showing genuine emotion (surprise, concern, excitement)
  • Red X / green checkmark visual language for “wrong vs. right“
  • Rural, authentic backdrops (not studio setups)

What to avoid

  • Pure clickbait that doesn’t deliver
  • Corporate stock imagery
  • Over-polished graphics that feel disconnected from the brand

Live streams and podcasts

Tone: Relaxed, chatty, community-focused

Characteristics

  • More tangential and conversational than edited videos
  • Directly engages with viewer comments and questions
  • Shares personal updates (birthday, family, travel)
  • Willing to say “I don’t know“ or “I’m not going to comment on that”
  • Inside jokes and callbacks for regular viewers
  • Longer explanations and deeper dives on topics

Website copy

Tone: Confident, supportive, and unmistakably Laurence. The website needs more structure than video content, but it should still sound like Laurence is talking to you. Don‘t sanitize the personality out in pursuit of “professional“ copy.

Characteristics:

  • Acknowledges reality first: Beekeeping is hard, things go wrong, most people are nervous starting out. Say it.
  • Then offers the solution Calm bees, ongoing support, no stupid questions.
  • Uses “I” and “we” naturally: “We’re here for you” not “Black Mountain Honey provides support“
  • Includes specific numbers: “Over 2,000 nucs sold,” “300lbs of honey,” “respond within 24 hours”—Laurence backs up claims with evidence
  • Strategic emphasis: CAPITALS for key points (sparingly), as Laurence does naturally
  • Maintains warmth: Even product pages should feel like you‘re being helped, not sold to

What to preserve from video/podcast voice

  • The self-deprecating honesty (“There are SO many things that can go wrong.…”)
  • The protective, mentor-like positioning (“We‘re here every step of the way“)
  • The directness (“We don‘t cut corners“)
  • Occasional exclamation marks for genuine enthusiasm (not hype)

What changes for web

  • More structured and scannable (headings, bullets, short paragraphs)
  • Slightly tighter—fewer tangents, get to the point faster
  • Clearer calls to action

Formula: Acknowledge the challenge > Show understanding > Present the solution > Reinforce ongoing support

Good vs. sanitised

Good

Starting out in beekeeping can be tough! There are SO many things that can go wrong. At Black Mountain Honey, we keep things simple. Calm, friendly bees. Seamless transactions. And we’re here for you every step of the way.

Sanitised

Black Mountain Honey offers premium nucleus colonies raised with expert care and delivered with comprehensive customer support.

Language and style guidelines

Do

  • Use first person (“I” for Laurence’s personal experience, “we” for the business)
  • Use second person (“you”) to speak directly to the audience
  • Use contractions in writing to make it appear conversational and personal (“we’re,” “it’s,” “you’ll’)
  • Include specific numbers and timeframes for credibility
  • Acknowledge challenges and mistakes openly
  • Use occasional mild profanity where it feels natural (not forced)
  • Use sentence case for headlines
  • Use CAPITALS for emphasis in key points only (sparingly)
  • Show genuine enthusiasm through word choice, not just exclamation marks

Don’t

  • Use corporate jargon or marketing speak
  • Oversell or make promises that can’t be backed up
  • Sound condescending or gatekeep-y about beekeeping knowledge
  • Use overly formal language or passive voice
  • Pretend to be perfect or hide mistakes
  • Use excessive exclamation marks (one per paragraph maximum)
  • Be vague when you can be specific

Signature phrases

  • “Every step of the way”
  • “Calm, placid, and prolific”
  • “We keep things simple”
  • “Direct from the hive”
  • “No-nonsense approach”
  • “We don’t cut corners”
  • “Unlimited support”
  • “Let me show you”
  • “This is the way I do it”
  • “Please don’t try this at home”
